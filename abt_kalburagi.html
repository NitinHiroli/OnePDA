Gulbarga, also and officially known as Kalaburagi,is a city in the Indian state of Karnataka. It is the administrative headquarters of Gulbarga district and is the largest city in the Hyderabad-Karnataka region of North Karnataka. Gulbarga is 623 km north of the state capital city of Bangalore and 220 km from Hyderabad.

Gulbarga city is called one of the Saint and Sufi cities having famous religious places, like Sri SharanaBasaveshwara Temple, Khwaja Banda Nawaz Dargah and Buddha Vihar. It also has a fort built during Bahmani rule. Has many domes like Hafth Gumbad (seven domes together) and Shor Gumbad. Gulbarga has a few architectural marvels built during the Bahamani Kingdom rule, including the Jama Masjid sited in the Gulbarga Fort.

Sri Sharanabasaveshwara Temple/Shrine

The temple is dedicated to an eminent Hindu religious teacher and philosopher, Shri Sharana Basaveshwara,a Lingayat saint of the 18th century known for his Dasoha (Giving is earning) and Kayaka.

The entire district is on the Deccan Plateau, and the elevation ranges from 300 to 750 m above MSL. Two main rivers, the Krishna and Bhima, flow through the district. The predominant soil type is black soil. The main crops are groundnuts, rice, and pulses.

Kalaburagi Toor dal

Gulbarga is the largest producer of toor dal, or pigeon peas, in Karnataka. Gulbarga is an industrially backward district but is showing signs of growth in the cement, textile, leather and chemical industries. Gulbarga has a university with Engineering and Medical Colleges.

ESIC Hospital & Medical College, Kalaburagi

It is a Central Government co-educational Medical College located at Sedam Road, Gulbarga. It is an MCI recognised medical college established in 2013 under the patronage of the ESI Corporation, a central autonomous body under Ministry of Labour and Employment, Government of India. The institute is under the Administrative Control of the ESI Corporation. It is affiliated to Rajiv Gandhi University of Health Sciences, Bangalore. The College is attached to a 500-bedded Multi-Speciality Hospital which was built in 2013 but became operational in 2014. The campus is spread in over 75 acres and provide primary and tertiary healthcare facilities to the workers insured under the ESI corporation as well as non insured persons and is one of the tertiary care referral center for smaller ESI clinic and dispensaries.

Some of the Polytical Ministers from this land

Veerendra Patil

He was a senior Indian politician and was twice, the Chief Minister of Karnataka. He became Chief Minister for the first time from 1968–1971; and the second time was almost 18 years later, from 1989–1990.He died on 14 March 1997 in Bangalore.

Dharam Singh

He was an Indian politician who was the 17th Chief Minister of Karnataka and seven time MLA from the Jevargi and former Member of Parliament from Bidar.He died on 27 July 2017 due to cardiac arrest in Bengaluru, aged 80.

Qamar ul Islam

He was an Indian politician who was the secretary of All India Congress Committee (AICC) and incharge of Kerala state. He was a former Member of Parliament. He completed his Bachelor's in Mechanical Engineering from PDA College of Engineering, Kalaburagi. He first stood in elections in PDA and became the president of the students' union, becoming the first and last Muslim student to hold the post of students' union president in PDA College. Qamar ul Islam died on 18 September 2017 at a hospital in Bengaluru due to cardiogenic shock and multi organ failure.

Umesh G. Jadav

He is an Indian politician and a Member of Parliament in the 17th Lok Sabha from the Gulbarga Lok Sabha constituency.

Mallikarjun Kharge

He is an Indian politician and was the leader of the Indian National Congress party in the 16th Lok Sabha. He is the former Minister of Railways and Minister of Labour and Employment in the Government of India.

Basavaraj Patil Sedam

popularly Known as Sedamji Indian politician who was the Member of Parliament in the Rajya Sabha for Karnataka. He is a Patron Of Bharat Vikas Sangam and He is brought up in the severe atmosphere of Tarnahalli, in the midst of god fearing, nature-loving agriculturists.

Dattatraya C. Patil Revoor

He is the son of Chandrashekar patil revoor EX MLA of Gulbarga Dakshina, He also known as the Appu Gouda Patil is an Indian politician and who is serving as Member of the Legislative Assembly for the Gulbarga Dakshina constituency.
